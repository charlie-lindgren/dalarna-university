# RV1050

## Lärandemål

Kursens övergripande mål är att studenten skall utveckla grundläggande kunskaper, färdigheter och förhållningssätt för att kunna hantera enklare EU-rättsliga frågeställningar i praktiken eller vid fortsatta studier.  
  
Efter avslutad kurs skall studenten kunna

  - _förklara_ väsentliga aspekter av den Europeiska unionen, EU-rätten och den EU-rättsliga metoden; detta inbegriper förmågan att kunna återge innehållet i centrala regler och principer och att kunna definiera vissa centrala begrepp; (1)

  - _identifiera_ och _analysera_ EU-rättsliga frågeställningar; detta inbegriper förmågan att kunna urskilja och beskriva EU-rätten i en given frågeställning; (2)

  - _tillämpa_ EU-rätten på en ny och konkret frågeställning; detta inbegriper förmågan att kunna använda EU:s samlade regelverk för att lösa EU-rättsliga problem; (3)

  - _värdera _EU-rättsliga regler och argument; detta inbegriper förmågan att kritiskt kunna reflektera över EU-rätten. (4)

## English Version

### Learning Outcomes

The overall aim of the course is for students to develop knowledge, skills and attitudes as a basis for dealing with issues related to EU law in theory and in practice.

On completion of the course, students shall be able to

  - explain the essential aspects of the European Union, EU law and methods related to EU law; this includes the ability to account for fundamental definitions, rules and prin-ciples of EU law; (1)

  - identify and analyze issues related to EU law; this includes the ability to detect and describe legal aspects of the European Union in a given question; (2)

  - apply the regulatory framework of the EU in order to solve problems related to EU law; this includes the ability to apply legal aspects of the European Union to a new and concrete question; (3)

  - evaluate rules and arguments related to EU law; this includes the ability to critically reflect upon the legal aspects of the European Union. (4)

### Course Content

The course deals with the essential aspects of the European Union, EU law and methods related to EU law. The emphasis of the course is placed on the following questions:

  - How power is shared between the Member States and the EU.

  - Who runs the EU; the institutional structure.

  - The sources of EU law.

  - The legal status of the EU and the alleged democratic deficit.

  - Basic principles that govern the relationship between EU law and national law.

  - The role and function of the Court of Justice of the EU.

  - The internal market, especially the free movement of goods.

Other aspects will be examined as well, for instance the brief history of the EU, legislative procedure and enforcement, fundamental rights and some selected policy areas of the EU, such as the economic and monetary union and competition policy.

### Assessment

Written exam (4.5 credits) as well as active participation in seminars including an assignment (3 credits). The written exam covers mainly learning outcomes (1) and (3). The seminars and the assignment cover mainly learning outcomes (2) and (4).

### Forms of Study

The teaching methods are lectures; mandatory seminars, where classic cases of the Court of Justice of the EU are discussed; and exercises, where study questions are discussed. A question and answer session before and a review after the written exam may occur.
